A felony is a serious crime that carries a potential sentence of imprisonment for more than one year or death. In Kentucky, felonies are classified by capital offenses and Classes A, B, C, and D.

*   **Class A**: 20 to 50 years, or life.
*   **Class B**: 10 to 20 years.
*   **Class C**: 5 to 10 years.
*   **Class D**: 1 to 5 years.

Conviction of a felony results in the loss of certain civil rights, such as the right to vote and own firearms.
